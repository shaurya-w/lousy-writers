# Subject: APPLIED MATHEMATICS

## Marks Allocation
125 marks  
UT 1 and UT 2: 20 + 20  
Term work: 25  
End semester: 60  

## Books or References
G.V. Kumbhojkar available in the library or online, avoid buying it. Go through the solved examples from the book and you’re good to go. Don’t go for the exercises in this book, it would be really time consuming. For UTs, solve the question banks that will be provided by the faculty. Personally, for end semester, we had 5 to 6 days study leave since maths is the first paper so I solved the solved examples from Kumbhojkar during that time and some PYQs.

# Subject: BASIC ELECTRONICS AND ELECTRICAL ENGINEERING (BEEE)

## Marks Allocation
150 marks  
UT 1 and UT 2: 20 + 20  
Term work: 25  
End semester: 60  
Viva: 25  

## Books or References
Ravi Singh available in library good solved examples for AC DC sums. Matthew sir’s notes for transformers, motors and other theoretical topics. PYQs practice.

## Note
It is a subject where some people lose a lot of marks even after studying so practice as much as you can. This is the subject where I would advise not doing things last minute. For viva, the professor or external can ask anything from the syllabus even tricky questions so make sure your concepts are clear. You will often be given a choice: they will ask which one’s your strongest topic for the viva so you can rely on that too.

# Subject: C PROGRAMMING

## Marks Allocation
50 marks  
Practical exam and viva: 25  
Term work: 25  

## Practical Exam
There will be a list of experiments that will be given to you from which you will be expected to execute a program on the day of the practical exam. Our faculty had chits for all the programs and you had to choose one from it. I had gotten sorting of a structure, by the way.

## Note
Study C properly as it will be a required prerequisite for many things that will come up in semester 2 as well as 3. As long as you keep up with the labs and practice before practical exam, we’re good to go.
