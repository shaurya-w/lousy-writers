I don’t know, maybe it was just me, or maybe everyone is equally giddy when they first learn to drive a car.

In my head, driving was cinematic: highway, wind rushing, music blasting, adrenaline high.

I wasn’t starting from zero either. I had been riding an Activa for a while, so I had some road sense. My father taught me that, but this time he insisted I join a proper driving school.

---

## Day 1: The Basics

The instructor gave a quick introduction to how a car works.

> “Listen to your vehicle.”

That stuck with me. The car tells you when something’s off — especially with gear shifts.

---

## The Struggle: Pickup

Pickup is everything.

It’s like balancing a switch exactly in the middle.

Too fast → stall  
Too slow → stall  

On Day 2, I tried:

- clutch pressed  
- gear in first  
- slight accelerator  
- slow release  

And it worked.

The car moved.

I had the biggest grin on my face.

---

## Small Wins

As days passed:

“I reached 4th gear today!”

That became a daily update at home.

---

## The Coastal Road Incident

On Day 10, my dad let me drive.

He sat beside me, hand hovering near the handbrake — just in case.

The drive started smooth.

Then came reality.

Signal. First in line. Green light.

Stall.

Again.

Stall.

Again.

Stall.

By now, the honking behind me turned into a full orchestra.

> Not the kind of main character moment you want.

We switched seats.

That one hurt a bit.

---

## Back to Practice

Training continued:

- longer routes  
- bridges  
- half-clutch control  
- reverse driving  
- angular parking  
- parallel parking  

I’ve improved a lot since then.

Parallel parking though… still a work in progress.

---

## The Driving Test

They say:

> If you can drive in India, you can drive anywhere.

Not sure if that’s backed by data, but it *feels* true.

My test was simple:

- examiner in passenger seat  
- drive in a circle  
- cones on both sides  

That’s it.

---

## Final Thought

Once the license arrives, this side quest is officially complete.

And yes, I *will* be saying:

> “I got my driver’s license last week.”
