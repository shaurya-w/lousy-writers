## Subject: Analysis of Algorithms

This subject was one of the most important this semester. It covered fundamentals of writing algorithms, starting from time and space complexity to sorting algorithms, and eventually introducing Greedy and Dynamic Programming.

It’s interesting to think all of this happens behind the scenes when you run a simple Ctrl + F.

Thinking about how solutions evolve from brute force to optimal approaches was new. Brute force feels intuitive, but not efficient. Seeing optimized solutions makes you wonder how they were even discovered.

The lectures were followed by practicals and weekly vivas. This is where the “analysis” part really came alive—questions ranged from complexities to best, average, and worst cases.

> Abdul Bari on YouTube is a lifesaver for algorithms and dry runs.

For reference:  
https://github.com/shaurya-w/AOA_codes/
